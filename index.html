Frequently Asked Questions.

What will it cost?

The construction cost will depend on many factors such as geography, square footage, type of building, building and finishing materials, and fixtures, to name a few.

Will I need a building permit?

Most jurisdictions will require a building permit for construction and renovations. At Raphco constructions we are be able to let you know which permits that your project will need before commencing construction. Generally, the contractor is responsible for obtaining the necessary permits, and the bid will usually include the cost.

How long will it take?

The length of time to complete your construction project will depend on the scope of the project. For instance, at Raphco, building a new restaurant from the ground-up takes about 6 months, while a commercial office building could take up to eight months or more. Also, delays in the permitting process can impact construction time. However, advancements in technology and construction methods are significantly shortening build times.

Who handles the design?

When you choose us as a design-build contractor, we will handle the project design either with an in-house architect or an established design partner. It streamlines communication and helps speed up the construction process. You also have the option of using an architect of your choice to work with your us.

How long have you been in business?

we have over 10 years experience. Understanding the length of time that a commercial contractor has been in business is an important question to ask. It will help ensure the contractor has the experience in building and managing the type of construction project you are planning to undertake.

Will you be subcontracting any of the work?

While the we at RapHco may perform most of the construction work, we will also likely use a variety of sub-contractors that have expertise in plumbing, electrical works, landscaping, and other specialties that your construction or renovation may require. We will schedule and supervise the subcontractors while they are working on the building site.
